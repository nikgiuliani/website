Adonis

This was an idea I had this summer. I felt unsatisfied with current diet and fitness applications; normally, you have to have an application for each and they're not really geared for tracking progress and growth. Along with that, I personally have a struggle with the motivation to exercise and feel as though I want it to be more engaging and interactive to me. I wanted to feel like exercise wanted me to participate in it, in a weird way; I wanted exercise to be fun and motivational.

So, I decided to create an application that gamifies fitness and progress called Adonis. Currently in the early phases, Adonis eventually will track fitness and diet in a Duolingo-esque manner, providing levels of progress and encouraging growth, a section to input your progress for both fitness and diet, and the option to see how one's diet and exercise directly affect one another. Eventually, I want to grow the project to include other areas of health, such as mental health. But for now, the project is envisioned to be what I explained above. I feel like this idea could be a good one and do a lot of good for health promotion for those like me who just need a little extra lift and motivation. Demo incoming in the near future!

TALL Embark

Embark is a language learning mobile and desktop application used by missionaries of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. I was privileged to be a member of the team on this application at the Missionary Training Center. While on the team, I created a variety of features ranging from new pages, alerts, and UI to entirely new features such as adding the option to redo an activity with only the questions the user got wrong. Before I was on the team, the project had been working on changing its image to be more attractive to users, and I helped continue to make the product attractive with the features I added. I worked on this application for a year, and during this time, it has reached tens of thousands of users learning dozens of languages and counting and has grown in popularity by over 10%.
